\section{Problem}
(GATE ME-2014 Q 4-ME section) A box contains 25 parts of which 10 are defective. Two parts are being drawn simultaenously in a random manner from the box. The probability of both parts being good is\\
$(A)\frac{7}{20}\ \ \ (B)\frac{42}{125}\ \ \ (C)\frac{25}{29}\ \ \ (D)\frac{5}{9}$
\section{solution}
Let $X_1,X_2\in\{0,1\}$ represent the parts, where $0$ represents good part, $1$ represent defective part. From the given information
\begin{align}
\pr{X_1=0}=\frac{15}{25}=\frac{3}{5}\\
\pr{X_2=0|X_1=0}=\frac{14}{24}=\frac{7}{12}
\end{align}
Then,
\begin{multline}
\pr{X_1=0,X_2=0}\\
=\pr{X_2=0|X_1=0}\times \pr{X_1=0}=\frac{7}{20}
\end{multline}
